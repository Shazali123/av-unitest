## Design Context

### Users
- **TA1 – Security Researchers & Malware Analysts**: Use AV-Unitest as a modular, lightweight framework to objectively evaluate how modern antivirus engines respond to raw behavioral payloads, stateful anomalies, and simulated zero‑day threats. They care about signal‑rich technical detail, transparent scoring, and trustable methodology.
- **TA2 – System Administrators & IT Professionals**: Benchmark the hardware performance cost (CPU, RAM, Disk I/O) of free third‑party antivirus solutions against native built‑in protection when provisioning or standardizing Windows environments. They need quick, comparable insights, and clear indicators of “safe to deploy” vs “too heavy.”
- **TA3 – Open‑Source Community & Everyday Users**: Verify antivirus marketing claims themselves through a transparent, public web dashboard, and—because the project is GPL‑3.0—study the architecture and extend it with custom modules. They value understandability, honesty, and low barrier to entry.

### Brand Personality
- **Core traits**: Transparent, modular, honest.
- **Voice and tone**: Clear, calm, and technically competent. Explains concepts plainly without hype or marketing spin. Documentation and UI copy should favor factual language and avoid fear‑mongering.
- **Emotional goals**: Users should feel informed and in control—confident they are seeing real, unbiased results they can verify or reproduce, not opaque “black box” scores.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Clean, minimalist, and modern with a security / technical feel, but without “gimmicky hacker” tropes. UI should feel like a serious benchmark tool, not a marketing site.
- **AI / emoji usage**: Avoid overt “AI‑generated” aesthetics, heavy emoji usage, or overly playful illustration. Use icons and symbols only where they aid scanning and meaning.
- **Layout**: Focused, data‑driven dashboard layout with clear hierarchy: hero (download & primary explanation) → global results overview → detailed tables / per‑run insights. Navigation must stay simple and predictable.
- **Theme**: Support both **light and dark** themes. Dark mode can lean into the existing deep blue / charcoal technical look; light mode should be high‑contrast, neutral, and comfortable for long reading.
- **Color palette**:
  - Base preference: black / white and neutral grays for most surfaces and typography.
  - Semantic colors: restrained use of **green** (good / high score), **red** (poor / failed), and possibly **amber** as a middle state. These should stand out against otherwise neutral UI so benchmark results and rankings “pop.”
  - Avoid visually noisy gradients, rainbow palettes, or oversaturated accent colors that compete with data.
- **Motion & effects**: Subtle, security‑tool‑appropriate motion—small hover states, gentle transitions for modals and accordions, lightweight skeleton loading. No splashy or distracting animations.

### Design Principles
1. **Data First, Decoration Second**: Prioritize clear presentation of scores, rankings, and performance metrics. Visual treatments should support comprehension and comparison, never obscure it.
2. **Transparent by Default**: Wherever possible, show how scores are derived (formulas, component breakdowns, timestamps) and avoid black‑box results. Copy should be honest and plainly worded.
3. **Minimal, Calm Interface**: Keep layouts uncluttered, with ample spacing, consistent typography, and limited color usage so users can scan quickly without fatigue.
4. **Serious but Approachable Security**: Present AV‑Unitest as a professional tool that everyday users can still understand—no fear‑based language, no hacker theatrics, but also no childish or playful visuals.
5. **Accessible & Responsive**: Aim for solid contrast and keyboard / screen‑reader friendly structures, and ensure the interface adapts cleanly to different screen sizes without breaking the hierarchy of information.
